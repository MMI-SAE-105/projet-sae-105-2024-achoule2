GLOSSARY

Here is a list of the most commonly used terms in the field of drones, to better understand the concepts discussed on this site.

DRONE

Unmanned flying device, remotely controlled or autonomously operated, used for various civil and military applications, ranging from surveillance to parcel delivery.

EMBEDDED SYSTEM

Set of electronic components and software integrated into a device (like a drone) allowing control of its functions and real-time data processing.

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE (AI)

Computer technology capable of analyzing data, learning, and making autonomous decisions based on real-time data analysis.

AUGMENTED REALITY (AR)

Technology overlaying digital information (images, sound, etc.) onto the reality perceived by the user, used in some drone control systems to offer an optimized view.

ROBOTICS

Branch of technology dealing with the design, construction, and use of robots, including drones.

LOITERING MUNITION

Type of military drone designed to remain stationary in flight before striking a specific target, also known as a "kamikaze drone".

AERIAL SURVEILLANCE

Use of drones to collect images and data from the air to monitor areas, infrastructure, or populations.

PRECISION AGRICULTURE

Application of drones to monitor and manage crops, allowing farmers to optimize yields using precise data on soil and plant conditions.

FPV (FIRST PERSON VIEW)

Drone control system using an onboard camera to transmit a real-time view to the pilot, providing full immersion as if the pilot were on board the drone.

LIDAR (LIGHT DETECTION AND RANGING)

Remote sensing technology using laser beams to create 3D images of environments, often used by drones for terrain mapping.

UAV (UNMANNED AERIAL VEHICLE)

Acroynm for Unmanned Aerial Vehicle, synonymous with drone, used for both civil and military applications.

AIR REGULATIONS

Set of laws and rules governing drone use in airspace to ensure the safety of operations and the protection of people.

REMOTE PILOTING

Action of controlling a drone remotely via a remote control or digital device, allowing it to be directed and maneuvered safely.
